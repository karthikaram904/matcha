About the Workshop
More than a class —
a lived experience

Our workshops are intimate, unhurried gatherings where you learn chado — the Way of Tea — through practice, not theory. Every session is capped at 12 people so our sensei can give you personal guidance.

Whether you join us in Chennai or online from anywhere in the world, you leave with a tangible skill: the ability to prepare and serve a bowl of ceremonial matcha with intention and care.

Your Instructor
Anika Menon
Certified Chado Practitioner · Educator

Anika trained under a certified chado sensei in Kyoto for three years before returning to India. She has led over 200 workshops and believes the ceremony is as important as the matcha itself.

Her teaching style is warm, unhurried, and deeply informed by the four principles of chado: harmony, respect, purity, and tranquility.

Curriculum
What you will learn

Six modules. Each one builds on the last — from the philosophy of the space to the final three sips.

01
Space & Intention

Why the physical environment matters in chado. How to cleanse and arrange utensils with mindful attention before the ceremony begins.

Philosophy
02
Water & Temperature

The science and art of heating water to 75–80°C. Understanding why temperature transforms the flavour of ceremonial matcha.

Technique
03
Sifting & Measurement

Correct use of the chashaku scoop and sifter. Why 2g is the traditional measure and how sifting affects foam quality.

Technique
04
Whisking Form

The W-motion technique. Wrist mechanics, chasen care, and how to build a stable, creamy foam that holds for several minutes.

Practice
05
Serving & Receiving

The etiquette of presenting the bowl — rotation, gesture, and the three deliberate sips of the chado tradition.

Etiquette
06
Daily Practice

How to bring chado into your morning ritual at home — simplified without losing meaning. Equipment care and storage guidance.
