Our History
A journey rooted
in 1482

"The first cup moistens my lips and throat. The second cup breaks my loneliness."

— Lu Tong, Tang dynasty

1482
Uji matcha is born

The Yamashiro region of Uji cultivates the world's first dedicated tencha crop.

1600s
Chado formalised

Sen no Rikyu codifies the Way of Tea — four principles: harmony, respect, purity.

2019
Chado Matcha founded

After a pilgrimage through Uji, we signed our first direct-farm partnership.

2025
8,000+ subscribers

Three farm partnerships, pan-India delivery, and a community of tea.

Why Choose Us
Not just matcha —
a whole practice

We don't just source matcha. We source the farmers, the philosophy, and the ceremony. Every decision we make traces back to one question: does this honour the leaf?

Learn our approach →
01
Direct from farm

We visit every farm personally. No brokers, no blending, no mystery origin stories — just a farmer's name and a harvest date.

02
Stone-ground only

Industrial mills generate heat that degrades L-theanine and catechins. We insist on traditional granite millstones — slower, costlier, better.

03
72-hour freshness

Matcha oxidises fast. We air-seal every tin within 72 hours of grinding and ship directly — no warehouse, no ageing on shelves.

04
Community & ceremony

Monthly workshops, harvest notes, and a growing community of practitioners. We teach the ritual, not just sell the ingredient.
