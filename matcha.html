What Is Matcha
More than a drink —
a way of life

Matcha (抹茶) literally means "powdered tea." Unlike regular green tea where leaves are steeped and discarded, matcha is made by grinding whole shade-grown tea leaves into a fine powder — so when you drink it, you consume the entire leaf.

The result is a uniquely rich, creamy, and complex drink with ten times the nutrients of regular green tea. Every sip delivers the full spectrum of the leaf's amino acids, antioxidants, and slow-release caffeine.

How to Prepare
Four steps to a
perfect bowl

The traditional preparation takes under five minutes. Each step matters — here is why.

1
Sift the matcha

Use a fine mesh sifter to sift 2g (one chashaku scoop) of matcha into your warm chawan bowl. Sifting breaks any clumps and ensures the powder disperses evenly in water — no lumps, no waste.

Warm the bowl first with hot water, then discard before sifting.
2
Add water at 75–80°C

Pour 60–70ml of water heated to 75–80°C over the matcha. Never use boiling water — 100°C scorches the amino acids and destroys the sweet umami character, leaving only bitterness.

Boil water first, then let it rest 2–3 minutes to reach 78°C.
3
Whisk in a W-motion

Using your chasen whisk, make brisk W or M-shaped strokes across the bottom of the bowl — not circular. Continue for 20–30 seconds until a thick, creamy foam forms on the surface.

Your wrist moves, not your arm. Keep the whisk tips close to the bowl base.
4
Receive and sip

In chado tradition, rotate the bowl twice before drinking to appreciate its form. Take three slow, deliberate sips. The first reveals bitterness. The second, sweetness. The third, the lingering finish.

Drink within 2–3 minutes of whisking before the foam settles.

Pro tip

For a latte, prepare a thin usucha bowl first (use 1g matcha, 40ml water), then pour over warm frothed milk. Never add cold milk directly — it prevents proper foam formation.

FAQ
Common questions
What temperature should I use for ceremonial matcha?

75–80°C is ideal. Boiling water (100°C) burns the amino acids responsible for sweetness and umami, leaving only bitterness. Let boiled water cool for 2–3 minutes, or use a temperature-controlled kettle.

How long does matcha stay fresh after opening?

Consume within 4–6 weeks of opening for peak flavour. Store in a cool, dark place with the tin lid sealed tightly. Do not refrigerate after opening — condensation will accelerate oxidation.

What is the difference between the three grades?

Ceremonial grade (first harvest) is for drinking straight — pure bowl preparation with water only. Premium grade suits lattes and daily drinking. Culinary grade is designed for baking, cooking, and blending where its boldness holds through heat and other ingredients.

How much caffeine does matcha contain?

A 2g serving of ceremonial matcha contains approximately 35–70mg of caffeine — similar to a shot of espresso. However, because of L-theanine, the effect is very different: sustained and calm rather than spiked and jittery.

Can I drink matcha every day?

Yes — 1–2 cups daily is the typical recommendation. Japanese tea practitioners often drink matcha twice a day as part of their morning and afternoon rituals. If you are sensitive to caffeine, avoid drinking after 3pm.
